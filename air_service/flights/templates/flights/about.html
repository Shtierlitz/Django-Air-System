About Django-Air-Service

DjangoAirSystem is a comprehensive flight booking and management platform designed to simplify and streamline the process of searching, booking, and managing flights. Our goal is to provide a user-friendly experience while ensuring security and efficiency.

Key Features

  • User Authentication: Users can create an account, log in, and log out securely. The platform also offers social login functionality through Google.
  • Flight Search: Users can search for available flights based on their origin, destination, and desired travel dates.
  • Flight Booking: After finding a suitable flight, users can easily book their seats.
  • Booking Management: Users can view, modify, or cancel their flight bookings at any time.
  • Profile Management: Users can manage their personal information, including their name, email address, gender and phone number.
  • Email Verification: Upon registration, users receive an email to verify their email address, ensuring the security of their account.
  • Interactive Chat with Managers: Users can communicate with Check-in managers and Gate managers through an interactive chat feature, allowing them to undergo necessary checks and receive assistance during the check-in and boarding processes.
  • Email Ticket Confirmation: After booking, users receive an email with an attached PDF ticket. They can also view or download the ticket on the ticket page within the platform.

Technologies Used

  • Django: DjangoAirSystem is built using Django, a high-level Python web framework that encourages rapid development and clean, pragmatic design.
  • Bootstrap: The platform utilizes Bootstrap, a popular front-end framework for developing responsive, mobile-first websites.
  • SQLite: SQLite is used as the database for storing flight and user data in the local host server.
  • PostgreSQL: PostgreSQL is used as the database for storing flight and user data in the development server.
  • AllAuth: Django AllAuth is used for handling user authentication, including social logins.
  • WebSockets and Django Channels: WebSockets and Django Channels are used for real-time communication between users and operators during the check-in and onboard control process.
  • Celery: Celery is used to create and manage tasks for handling ticket expiration, ensuring timely notifications and updates for users regarding their bookings.

Future Enhancements

We plan to continually improve and expand DjangoAirSystem to provide an even better experience for our users. Some of our planned enhancements include:

  • Adding more filter options for flight search, such as filtering by airline, duration, or layovers.
  • Implementing a loyalty program that rewards frequent flyers with points and exclusive discounts.
  • Integrating more social login options, such as Facebook and Twitter.
  • Expanding our database to include more airlines and flight routes.
  • Developing a mobile app for iOS and Android devices.
